Do the Job Right With Plumbing Tools


With the right plumbing tools and equipment, you can replace leaky sink pipes or even install a brand new toilet. Cutting new drain pipes is easier with a PVC cutter. Choose a ratcheting cutter to make cutting pipe effortless. Join together pipes and fittings with the appropriate plumbing primer and cement to prevent leaks.


Use a deburring tool to smooth the edges of freshly cut pipes before installing them. There are specific plumbing tools and wrenches for replacing drains and faucets. For loosening and tightening hard-to-reach nuts on faucets and toilets, use a basin wrench. If you need to install or replace a tub drain, a drain plug wrench will make the job much simpler.


Use a toilet jack to help with replacing a toilet. These jacks make maneuvering a heavy toilet easier and safer. You'll need a new wax ring and you may need to replace the flange. For step-by-step help, see our guide — How to Replace a Toilet.
