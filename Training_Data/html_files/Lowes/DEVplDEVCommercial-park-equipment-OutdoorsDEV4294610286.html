Frequently Asked Questions


Q: Is there a standard size for picnic tables?

A: The standard size for a stationary picnic table that seats six people is 72 inches long, 59 inches wide and 30 inches tall, with benches that are 17 inches tall. But picnic tables come in many dimensions. Square and circular picnic tables are around the same height as the standard, but they offer different seating configurations. Other options include folding picnic tables, which are often smaller than the standard size.


Q: How long do wooden picnic tables last?

A: Wooden picnic tables can last up to 20 years, but longevity depends on many factors, including a tables construction and quality, its use and care, and the climate. If its important for your picnic tables to last a long time, consider tables in hardwoods, such as teak or cedar, or those treated for durability. Additionally, clean your tables regularly, protect them from harsh weather and do routine maintenance.


While wooden picnic tables are the most familiar, you might consider other options that can stand up to extreme use and conditions. Picnic tables with wooden planking and a steel frame help blend a traditional look with a sturdy structure, and thermoplastic-covered steel picnic tables can take heavy use and exposure to the elements with little maintenance. Plastic picnic tables are a good, lightweight option that resists weather damage and can offer portability.
