Be Safe at Home and on the Job With Safety Gear From Lowe’s 

Being safe at home and on the job requires the right safety gear and equipment. Lowe’s is here to provide you with everything you need to keep you protected and productive, from on-the-job safety wear and worksite tools to home medical kits, safety detectors and more.  


At Home 

Smoke Detectors: To keep your home safe from dangers like fires, fire safety equipment is essential. Smoke detectors are an important addition to your home, and the best practice is to place them in each room of your house. Also consider having fire extinguishers for fire defense in the kitchen and adding carbon monoxide detectors for added safety.


Baby Monitors: Ensure your child’s safety and well-being with baby monitors. Whether they’re sleeping or playing, you’ll be able to check in. Choose from handheld or Wi-Fi options, depending on your needs.  

Driveway Markers: If your driveway entrance is hard to see, flagging it with driveway markers is a great way to increase its visibility, especially at night or in bad weather.  


For Projects at Home or the Jobsite

Head Protection: We’ve got an assortment of hard hats for sale, a must-have accessory for construction work. They’re designed to protect your head from injury from falling objects or bumping into fixed objects around the site.


Face Protection: Eye safety is important, no matter the project you’re taking on. From specific brands, like Oakley safety glasses, to antifog safety glasses — which are designed to keep your vision clear in hot and humid conditions — you can find the perfect pair to protect your eyes. Facial shields not only protect your eyes, but protect the rest of your face from splashes, splatters and other debris. 

Hand Protection: Work gloves protect your hands from injury. The best work gloves for you depend on the task. We carry leather work gloves, which help defend against cuts and abrasions, rubber gloves, garden gloves and more, so you can choose the right pair for your activity.  

Other Body Protection: Your projects may extend into the evening and night, and to ensure you stay visible, a reflective vest should be part of your workwear. Opt for a safety vest with pockets so you can keep things like pens, your phone and more, handy. For jobs like installing flooring, knee pads help ease aches and pains. 

Worksite Safety: If you work on large construction sites, you may need to draw attention to hazardous areas or redirect traffic, and traffic cones, like orange cones, can easily create a clear boundary. Caution tape is another way to mark places that may be unsafe. 

Working and living safely are important, and however you need to add safety precautions into your life, Lowe’s has what you need to create safe atmospheres. Shop for your safety gear on Lowes.com and pick up at a store near you for convenience.  


Frequently Asked Questions 


What should I include in a basic first aid kit?

First aid kits range from simple to complex — depending on how much work you put into creating them. Any good first aid kit will include compresses, adhesive bandages, antibiotic ointments, antiseptic wipes, gauze pads, a thermometer and tweezers, among other essentials. If you’d prefer to purchase one, we have several first aid kits available at Lowe’s.


What are safety glasses used for?

Safety glasses are used to protect eyes from irritants like wood particles, dirt, harmful fumes, excess UV-light exposure and more. To avoid eye damage, including blindness, always wear safety glasses for jobs like construction, welding, carpentry and yardwork.


Where should carbon monoxide detectors be placed in my home?

Carbon monoxide detectors should be placed on each level of your home, near every bedroom or sleeping area as well as near attached garages and basements. They should be placed five feet from the ground, where they receive the best reading of the air inside your home.
