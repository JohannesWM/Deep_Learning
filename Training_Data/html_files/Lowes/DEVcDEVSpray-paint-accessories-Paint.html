Spray-Paint Your Way to New Looks and Protective Finishes

Using spray paint is a great way to breathe new life into your DIY paint projects. From crafts to cars and everything in between, there’s a spray paint that can add the perfect finishing touch and bring a little color to your world.

Types
There are different spray paints for different jobs. Working on an auto-related project? Car spray paint is ideal for the car itself, hitches, ladder racks and more. It offers a durable coat that withstands the wear and tear of vehicle use. You can even find more specific auto-related options like engine enamel and paints for wheel coating, etching and more. Add protection to metal surfaces like swing sets and bikes with spray paint that stops rust. All-surface and decorative options are typically good spray paints for plastic, so you can refresh those lawn chairs or your plastic mailbox. Primers and marking and striping spray paints, great for temporary applications like marking athletic fields, are also available.

Colors and Finishes
With a variety of spray paint colors to choose from, you can easily find the ideal hue your project needs — vibrant blues, bold reds, muted grays and everything in between. Once you’ve found your color, consider the kind of finish you want. A matte or flat finish covers imperfections and leaves a smooth, no-shine look on the surface. Satin offers a slight shine, while semi-gloss, gloss and high-gloss options reflect more and more light, providing a bright, polished look. Maybe you’re turning a few of your kitchen cabinet fronts into a chalkboard for grocery lists. Or perhaps you’re revamping a picture frame or upcycling votives. For any of these projects and many more, you may want to opt for specialty finishes like chalkboard, glitter or metallics to achieve the perfect look.

Whether you’re updating old lamps, repurposing a dresser, transforming a picnic table or protecting your metal patio furniture, Lowe’s is here to see your project through with trusted spray paint brands like Rust-Oleum and Krylon®. Brush up on spray paint basics with our helpful guide and learn the proper technique for using spray paint, prepping the surface and more. And don’t forget to shop for spray paint accessories, like a spray paint gun, to make the application even easier.
