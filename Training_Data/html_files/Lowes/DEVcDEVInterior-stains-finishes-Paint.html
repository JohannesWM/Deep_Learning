Refresh Your Surfaces With Interior Stains and Finishes


Wood finishes and stains are an easy way to give old furniture and surfaces a new and improved look. There are several types of interior stains that can be applied to achieve different results. Not sure which product to choose for your do-it-yourself (DIY) stain projects? We’ll help you decide.


Interior Stain Types

Oil-based stains enhance wood surfaces with pre-mixed colors that penetrate the surface. They’re ideal for furniture and cabinets.


Water-based stains come in more than 200 shade options, from dark chocolate brown to bright red and everything in between. To get the exact color you want, you can visit the Lowe’s Paint Desk to get any stain tinted to your preference.


Gel stains are ideal for vertical surfaces thanks to their nondrip formula. Minwax® gel stain is meant for wood, veneer, fiberglass and other surfaces.


Stains and sealers in one offer a rich, long-lasting stain with the protection of polyurethane and the benefit of less work.


Cabinet stain is made with an oil-enriched formula that provides a strong factory-like finish. If you want a professional, high-end look, opt for cabinet and furniture stain for kitchen and bathroom cabinets. Valspar® cabinet stain is ideal for use on bare wood as well as previously painted wood, metal or prepared laminate.


Countertop paint provides a fast, easy and cost-effective way to transform outdated countertops into high-end granite look-alikes without the hassle of a complete replacement. We carry products that can resurface a variety of materials, including laminate, ceramic, tile, wood and more.


Interior Stain Colors

At Lowe’s, you can tint your stain to over 200 opacities, so the options are endless. Whether you need a simple black wood stain or a very specific shade of blue, you’ll get exactly what you need with the help of our Paint Desk.


Wood Oils and Wood Repair Options

Wood oil — like tung oil, teak oil and Danish oil — helps to seal and protect wood and keep it from cracking, peeling and chipping. Wood varnish is another option you can use to protect wood furniture. It’s a transparent protective layer that creates a hard outer surface with a glossy finish.


If your wood is damaged, we can help. Have a crack in a wood table? Need wood stain remover? We have every wood repair item you need, including wood fillers and putties, stain markers, and blend pencils.


Already have a brand of wood stain in mind? From Minwax® and WATCO to STAINMASTER™ and Mohawk, we carry all the top wood stain brands in a range of types, sizes, tints and more. Want to simplify the process? Pick up a countertop kit for resurfacing and painting kitchen countertops, with everything you need to get started. Need inspiration before you get started? Check out our Artfully Aged Wood Finishes or Refinish Wood Furniture how-to guides. You’ll get inspiration and tips for how to refresh your wood surfaces with stains and finishes.


Frequently Asked Questions


Q: What’s interior stain for?

A: Interior stain is a versatile product that can instantly transform dated floors or old furniture. You can use it on a variety of surfaces, including flooring, trim, cabinets and furniture.


Q: What’s the best way to apply interior stain? 

A: The best way to apply interior stain depends on the finish you’re looking to achieve. Typical application tools include paint rollers, foam brushes, paint sprayers and cleaning cloths. A paint roller is ideal for staining a deck or siding, while cleaning cloths work best for applying stain to smaller surfaces such as furniture.


Q: What are common types of wood stain? 

A: Common types of wood stain include:


  • Oil-based stain
  • Water-based stain
  • Gel stain
  • Stain and sealer in one
