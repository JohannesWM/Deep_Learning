Wet or Dry, Shop Vacuums Handle Any Kind of Mess

A handy item to have around your home, a shop vacuum can easily clean messy jobs ordinary vacuum cleaners can’t — like wood shavings on your workshop floor or wet spills. From cleaning your car to clearing ashes from a fireplace, a wet vacuum cleaner goes beyond the everyday mess, tackling a variety of tougher cleaning tasks with ease.


Shop Vacuum Options

The right shop vacuum for you depends on your needs. Options range from mini shop vacuums, available in handheld and canister designs and with tank capacities ranging from 2 to 6 gallons, to heavy-duty shop vacuums with tanks that can hold up to 18 gallons and clear both fine dust and large debris. If you find yourself cleaning messes in various areas around your home or workspace, consider investing in a portable shop vacuum. Its smaller size and lightweight design make it easy to move, while still offering the cleaning power you need. Cordless wet/dry vacuums extend the convenience of portability by ditching the power cord, giving you more freedom to move around the area while you clean.


The loudness of the machine is a factor to consider, too, and you can determine the noise level based on the decibel rating. A quiet shop vacuum will have a decibel rating of 70 or lower, meaning it won’t disrupt conversations in your garage or workshop. Want to make sure you’re trapping dirt, dust and other debris while vacuuming? The filters in shop vacuums are designed to keep those particles from recirculating in the air. Consider a HEPA shop vacuum if you want to capture at least 99% of dust mites and other allergens.


Accessories

Whatever you use your shop vacuum for, you’ll want to have the right parts and accessories to go with it. Look for shop vacuum attachments, like extension wands, crevice tools and multi-tool kits, for various applications. You’ll need to have extra shop vacuum collection bags for the dry debris you pick up and replacement shop vacuum filters to change out dirty ones when necessary. Shop vacuum hoses come in a variety of sizes, including 8-foot and 50-foot options, so you can extend your reach to suit the job.
