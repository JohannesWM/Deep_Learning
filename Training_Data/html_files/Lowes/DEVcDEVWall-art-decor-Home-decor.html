Search by Color

Another approach designers from all disciplines use is starting their project with a common color palette. While this may sound restrictive, it's actually a powerful creative technique. Let's say your bedroom is painted blue and green and you're looking for new wall décor options; try working with the blue hues of a peacock's iconic plume. Or, instead of thinking of primary versus complementary, use the colors of a sunrise as a palette for painting an accent wall. These explorations allow you to stay in your design comfort zone, but they also lead to discovering new perspectives, which in turn keeps your design eye fresh.
