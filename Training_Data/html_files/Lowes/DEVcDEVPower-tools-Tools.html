Find the Right Power Tools for Your Project


Power Tools for DIY Projects

Power tools are used for many day-to-day tasks, such as drilling or fastening, sanding, painting, heating and more. There are many different types of power tools and power tool combo kits to help your projects go smoothly. Whether you’re looking for the best oscillating tool or best rotary tool, Lowe’s has a great selection of high-quality power tools to choose from. Simply need a multi-tool, blades for your saw or a replacement battery to keep on hand? From tools to accessories, we have it.


Types of Power Tools

Saws
A circular saw can be used almost universally. It’s used for both crosscuts (against the grain) and rip cuts (with the grain). There are two kinds of circular saws: corded and cordless. The miter saw can make the same cuts as the circular saw while hitting more precise angles and comes in a blade diameter of 10 inches or 12 inches. These also come corded and cordless; however, they aren’t as portable as the circular saw.


Drills
There are two types of power drills: cordless and corded. The cordless drill offers a large range of options in terms of mobility and portability. It makes it easier to drill in tight spaces and awkward angles. The cordless power drill offers flexibility, but it uses a battery that has to be charged. You can find higher-voltage cordless power drills for more high-powered jobs. Corded drills offer more power but may require an extension cord, depending on the size of your workspace.


Rotary and Oscillating Tools
These versatile tools let you sand, cut and polish a variety of materials. Rotary tools spin bits at high speeds and can be used for etching glass, removing grout and more. Oscillating tools use a back-and-forth motion to cut, scrape, sand and grind. Some oscillating tool blades can cut drywall, scrape away paint, or sand and smooth wood.


Angle Grinders
Angle grinders are primarily used for grinding metal but can be used on wood as well. For denser materials, you’ll need a higher wattage; for large diameters and deeper cuts, you’ll need a larger disc. Having multiple speeds ensures proper precision when working with the angle grinder. They can come in power tool combo kits, and there are cordless options, as well.


Power Sanders
A power sander can finish the job more quickly and remove the harsher blemishes more efficiently than handheld sandpaper can. Power sanders are also used to prepare drywall for painting, and the orbital power sander is the best one for the job. These power tools come cordless or corded.


Power Tool Combo Kits
Purchasing a kit helps ensure you have all the basics. Combo kits are more affordable because they’re a bundle deal, with everything you’ll need for many jobs.


Accessories
Routers are especially vital with woodworking tasks. This power tool accessory rotates and hollows out the material. These can be cordless or corded and can come fixed, plunged and as an accessory to a stationary router table. When working with cordless power tools and power tool combo kits, it’s important to have extra batteries and battery chargers. There are a few options for battery types, including lithium, lithium-ion and nickel cadmium. Outperforming in all areas, lithium-ion is the best choice for rechargeable power tool batteries.


Frequently Asked Questions

What are the advantages and disadvantages of cordless power tools?
Corded power tools can run as long as you need them to if you have access to an electrical outlet. Cordless tools require a charged battery, which can be an issue, depending on the length of the job you’re completing. On the flip side, it’s much easier to move around a workspace or jobsite with a cord-free tool. Also, it’s good to know that, generally, corded power tools offer more power than cordless options.


What type of power tool is more powerful — cordless or corded?
Though there are some cordless power tools that offer efficient power, corded tools are known to deliver more consistent power.


Is a higher voltage better for power tools?
Think of voltage as a power tool’s horsepower. The higher the voltage of a power tool, the more work it can tackle. If you work on heavy-duty projects, look for power tools with high voltage ratings.
