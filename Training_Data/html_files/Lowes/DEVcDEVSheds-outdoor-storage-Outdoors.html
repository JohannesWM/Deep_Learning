Frequently Asked Questions


Q: What size shed do you need for a push lawn mower?


A: Small sheds, such as those up to 36 square feet, should be adequate to hold a push lawn mower. If you have other power equipment to store, then a medium-size shed up to 100 square feet will likely house it easily.


Q: What are the advantages and disadvantages of a metal shed?


A: Metal sheds are easy to maintain, thanks to their vinyl-coated steel or galvanized steel construction with an enamel finish to resist scratches and rust. They come in a variety of colors and are usually very easy to assemble. However, metal sheds may not be able to withstand high winds or heavy snows. Also, metal sheds may not be as attractive as wood or even plastic sheds.


Q: What are the advantages and disadvantages of a wood shed?


A: The lumber framing in a wood shed provides strength to withstand wind and snow and can support multiple shelves and hangers. You can paint these sheds to match your home or to blend in with your landscape. Wood sheds are more complex to assemble than metal or resin sheds. If you choose a wood shed, you’ll need to check it yearly to make sure there isn’t any rot or insect damage.
